### Important caveats (must factor into windowing decisions)

1. **OKX is brand new — started 2026-05-06 ~10:58 UTC.** Only ~1 hour of data at audit time.
   Despite being Tier-A, OKX cannot be included in the 4-day short window. Choices:
   - Wait until 2026-05-10 to get a full 4d OKX history, OR
   - Drop OKX from the short-window pool and rely on binance + kucoin as the Tier-A anchor.
2. **Long-window (≥60d) pool is thin.** Only `binance` and `bitget` and `gate` cover the full
   2.5-month window, and even there:
   - `binance ETH_USDT` first row is 2026-03-21 (~46d), so binance ETH **fails the 60d test**
     for the full 2.5mo window — would be 46d at most. (Other binance bases reach 2026-02-27.)
   - `bitget` BTC/ETH also start 2026-03-21, only SOL/BNB/XRP/DOGE go back to 2026-02-27.
   - `gate` is the only exchange with all 6 bases >60d.
   - Reasonable long-window plan: SOL/BNB/XRP/DOGE on (binance + bitget + gate) ≥ 60d.
     BTC requires (binance + gate) only at full 2.5mo.
     ETH ≥ 60d would require either (gate alone) or shrinking to ~45d window.
3. **Short-window (≥4d, 2026-05-02 → now) pool — the 8 exchanges per base** are
   binance, bitget, kucoin, mexc, gate, bingx, kraken, pyth (BTC/ETH/SOL only) plus pancake/aster/lighter/hyperliquid/okx as `OK_THIN` (<4d coverage). Window must start no earlier

4. **lighter has bid==ask in raw rows** (spread_bp = 0). Mid is fine but treat as quote-only;
   no spread information available.
5. **pyth bid/ask are conf-interval bounds**, not market-maker quotes. Median spread of

   reference-only — leave excluded from index, OK to include for diagnostic comparison.
